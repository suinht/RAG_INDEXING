What is RAG?

RAG stands for Retrieval-Augmented Generation. It's a technique used to make AI models better answers by adding updated facts from external sources or your #Knowledge-base.
RAG contains many technical and academic terminology explanations, which can be challenging for business professionals to understand quickly. Therefore, I sought a straightforward example for my first series related to data and AI.

To be honest, I was influenced by the CS50 course at Harvard, taught by David J. Malan when worked on the explanation.

Breaking Down RAG:

Indexing

  1. Chunking:
    • What is it? Chunking is like breaking a big book into smaller chapters for easier reading.
    • Why do it? It helps handle a lot of text more easily.
    • How does it work? We use special tools to split big documents into smaller parts. These parts are then encoded into a format that computers can understand better.
  1. Embedding:
    • What is it? Indexing is like making a list of all the chapters in a book along with their page numbers.
    • Why do it? It helps find specific information quickly.
    • How does it work? We convert the small text parts into numbers and store them in a database. When we want to find something, we compare these numbers to see which parts match what we want.

Retrieval

  1. Retrieval and Generation:
    • What is it? It's like finding the right book chapters and creating a new story using them.
    • Why do it? It helps generate accurate answers based on the information found.
    • How does it work? We use the indexed information to find what's needed. Then, we combine it with user questions or prompts to create accurate responses.

Business Application:
For businesses using AI, understanding Chunking and Indexing in RAG is important because it helps:

  • Find information quickly and accurately.
  • Make AI-generated answers more reliable.
  • Make better decisions by accessing the right information faster.
In short, by using Chunking and Indexing in RAG effectively, businesses can use AI to turn data into useful insights, leading to innovation and staying ahead in the market.
